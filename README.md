# The-Rainbow-Road-Humormanity

## OVERVIEW

In my McBride Honors Course, I took a class that examined public affairs and society through the lens of Humor. For our final project, we created arguments on the relationship of Humor and Humanity. My argument was that **the parallels between humor and trauma serve as pivotable intersections for queer discourse to transform humanity.** 

*How does the presentation of trauma in humor alter perspectives or reveal prejudices?*

*How does trauma in humor invoke different reactions and actions of humanity?*

To explore these questions, for my non-textual project, I recreated the existential queer experience, the “coming out” journey, in a game. In “Oregon-trail”-inspired fashion, players navigate the (potentially traumatic) queer experience. The game, like humor, presents queer trauma in different ways allowing the user to develop their own playthrough and opinion. By drawing on classic “Oregon Trail” symbolism, such as resilience in the face of hardship, constant risk, and the promise of reward, this comparison reframes the queer experience as a similarly difficult journey. The “rocky” (rainbow) road becomes a metaphor for navigating social discourse on queer communities using humor and trauma.
